# Style Map

Map each taxonomy category to one dominant visual language. Keep the direction stable and memorable.

## `concrete-objects`

- Label: Micro studio macro
- Direction: macro still-life photography, ultra high resolution, clean studio background
- Camera: macro lens or tight product close-up
- Focus: surface texture, polish, translucency, machining, reflective edges
- Lighting: controlled studio lighting, crisp specular highlights, neutral backdrop
- Avoid: cluttered props, messy environments, human drama overpowering the object

## `people-roles`

- Label: Cinematic environmental portrait
- Direction: environmental portraiture with story-bearing context
- Camera: 85mm medium shot, shallow depth of field
- Focus: face, gaze light, hands, tools, posture, social identity
- Lighting: sculpted key light with subtle background bokeh
- Avoid: generic stock portraits with no role-specific signals

## `spaces-architecture`

- Label: Atmospheric spatial photography
- Direction: ultra-wide architectural or city photography
- Camera: wide-angle view with strong perspective lines
- Focus: geometry, scale, structural rhythm, region-specific details
- Lighting: ambient occlusion, atmospheric depth, layered light falloff
- Avoid: empty game-like renders with no cultural specificity

## `nature-phenomena`

- Label: Epic documentary landscape
- Direction: National Geographic style environmental photography
- Camera: wide landscape framing
- Focus: ecology, weather, terrain, water, haze, scale
- Lighting: high dynamic range, realistic sky and terrain behavior
- Avoid: fantasy oversaturation or synthetic-looking skies

## `dynamic-actions`

- Label: High-speed impact frame
- Direction: high-speed action photography
- Camera: frozen mid-action framing with kinetic composition
- Focus: particles, impact, fragments, shock, muscle tension, projectile path
- Lighting: hard directional light, visible debris, optional motion blur at the edges
- Avoid: static poses pretending to be action

## `processes-transformations`

- Label: Time-lapse transformation
- Direction: concept-rich before/after or staged transition image
- Camera: stable frame or split composition that reveals change
- Focus: physical or chemical evolution, progression, emergence, decay, illumination
- Lighting: directional progression, one state transitioning into another
- Avoid: abstract fog with no readable transformation

## `abstract-society`

- Label: Editorial surreal metaphor
- Direction: New Yorker style editorial illustration or restrained surrealism
- Camera or medium: illustration-first, poster-like clarity, geometric metaphor
- Focus: symbolic structure, contradiction, systems, civic or philosophical meaning
- Lighting: graphic contrast, intelligent color restraint
- Avoid: empty symbolism, random protest posters, overused icons without insight

## `cross-cultural-historical`

- Label: Period drama cinematic frame
- Direction: historically grounded cinematic still
- Camera: medium-wide scene with clothing, props, architecture, and gesture
- Focus: era fidelity, cultural material detail, ceremonial or social atmosphere
- Lighting: cinematic but believable, tuned to the time and place
- Avoid: stereotypes, cosplay aesthetics, mixed-period inaccuracies

## `sensory-adjectives`

- Label: Synesthetic sensory art
- Direction: visually translate sound, smell, vividness, heat, softness, and intensity
- Camera or medium: hybrid image language, often photoreal subject plus stylized sensory cues
- Focus: color grading, wave distortion, scent trails, exaggerated texture
- Lighting: tuned to the dominant sense impression
- Avoid: vague abstraction with no anchor subject

## `social-interjections`

- Label: Expressive social reaction
- Direction: high-quality stylized 3D by default, pop-art when the word is punchy or comic
- Camera: medium shot with readable face and gesture
- Focus: expression, body language, relational energy, conversational beat
- Lighting: bright, inviting, emotionally legible
- Avoid: deadpan neutral poses or generic emoji-style simplification

## Medium Switch Notes

- Default to photorealistic output for categories 1 to 6 and most of 8.
- Default to illustration or surreal editorial art for category 7.
- Default to stylized 3D or pop-art for category 10.
- Category 9 can mix realism with stylized overlays, but one sensory anchor must remain concrete.
